Preamble

Langos is a traditional hungarian street food. It's litteraly 'flamer' or 'flamey' but can be called fried bread.

Ingredients

  • 300 g all-purpose flour (plus extra on hand)
  • 7 g dried (instant) yeast
  • 250 ml water
  • Â½ teaspoon salt
  • peanut oil for frying

Instructions

Making the dough

  1. Sift in the flour into a bowl.
  2. Mix salt and water together in a mug.
  3. Add the salt water to the flour
  4. Mix the dough and water. I recomend kneeding with your hands. Sprinkle flour in and keep kneeding until the dough is smooth and stops sticking to the bowl.
  5. Coat the bowl with flour so the dough doesn't stick. Cover with a clean cloth and leave it to rise for about 30-40 mins. You can also let is rise overnight in the fridge if cooking for an event and want to prepare earlier.

Preperation and cooking

  1. Once the dough has risen, cut pieces off, flatten in your hand and stretch them out. Or roll out on a floured surface and cut out pieces with a glass, then stretch. Aim for about 10cm diameter pieces. When stretching out, the middle should be thinner than the edges
  3. Leave pieces to rest another 30 mins on a floured surface. If the dough rested overnight,I found this step isn't really needed
  4. Heat up some oil - I use peanut oil, original recipe recomends sunflour oil
  5. Test the oil is hot enough with a small piece of dough. Then fry your langos. Approx 30 seconds per side, or however long is takes to get each side golden brown. Repeat with all for our langos

Serving

  1. Langos is best served hot. Or you can try heat it back up in the oven, but fresh is best
  2. Try it with...
    • Plain - maybe add some salt
    • Garlic - cut the end of a clove off, and make some extra serations. Rub on langos.
    • Cheese and sour cream - spread some sour cream, then spinkle grated tasty cheese on
